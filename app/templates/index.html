Made to Judge: How much can you trust AI?

AI has been becoming more and more commonplace in society and everyday life, from simple chatbots to facial recognition systems. One of the more consequential applications of AI is in the American judicial system. More specifically, machine learning models are being used as decision aids to help judges determine whether or not defendants are predicted to reoffend, influencing their decisions on bail and custody for the defendant. With such high stakes on the line, how much trust, as a society, can we put on these technologies? To gauge that, we must first understand how these models work. This app will walk you through creating your own ML model, and then see how it interacts with different types of people.
